Section 1 · Geo & Market Signals
Macro-level technology spending, economic, and regulatory signals across ITCI's four primary geographies.
1.1 · Americas
Signal
Worldwide IT spend projected at $6.31 trillion in 2026, +13.5% YoY (Gartner, Apr 2026), with IT services growing 8.7%. The 2025 US tariff regime raised the effective import rate to 7.7% — highest since 1947 (ISG, Mar 2025). H-1B annual salary minimum raised to $100,000+ via revised wage-tier lottery effective Sep 2025, materially increasing onsite delivery costs for Indian IT firms. $13B in IT contracts up for renewal by end-2025; 68% of CIOs plan a 20% reduction in vendor count (SAP CIO Survey, Aug 2025). Tariffs are tilting CPG and Manufacturing toward large cost-optimization and AI-led automation programs while smaller discretionary projects slow (ISG, Mar 2025). CPG AI adoption reached 71% across at least one function in 2024, up from 42% in 2023 (McKinsey/LatentView, 2024); 97% expect AI spend to increase next fiscal year (NVIDIA, Jan 2025).
ITCI Lens
ITCI's Americas-concentrated revenue base sits at the intersection of all four macro forces simultaneously. Tariff-driven cost pressure is not reducing IT spend in Manufacturing and CPG — large cost-optimization programs remain resilient. CPG AI spend is ring-fenced from cuts. The H-1B surcharge resets the onsite delivery cost baseline industry-wide for all Indian IT firms equally. The $13B renewal wave and 68% CIO vendor-reduction intention represent a structural procurement shift directly affecting mid-tier IT firms with multi-vertical Americas portfolios.
Sources: Gartner Apr 2026 · ISG Mar 2025 · SAP CIO Survey Aug 2025 · McKinsey/LatentView 2024 · NVIDIA Jan 2025
1.2 · Europe
Signal
European IT spend projected at $1.4 trillion in 2026, +11.1% (Gartner, 2026). EU AI Act enforcement: prohibited practices (Feb 2025, active), GPAI governance (Aug 2025, active), full high-risk compliance by Aug 2026 — fines of €35M or 7% of global turnover (European Commission AI Act, 2024). DORA took effect Jan 2025; 83% of surveyed European financial entities have formal cost estimates, 64% budgeting €2–5M per institution (Deloitte DORA Survey, 2025). 3,600+ German institutions responding (Fortinet, 2026). European banking cloud outsourcing rose 13.5% YoY to an average €57M per institution (ECB, Feb 2025). UK: 57% of organisations plan to increase outsourcing in 2025. SAP S/4HANA migrations active: Nestlé (112 countries), Unilever Clean Core, LVMH Google Cloud AI. SAP consulting rates projected to rise 30–50% in 2026–27 (IC Euro, 2025).
ITCI Lens
European CPG and BFSI are generating the most immediate IT services demand. The SAP migration wave creates sustained multi-year program work with consulting talent tightening and rates rising through 2027. The EU AI Act August 2026 deadline creates mandatory compliance spend in BFSI and Manufacturing — formally budget-committed by 83% of European institutions surveyed. European banking cloud adoption and UK outsourcing expansion signal live procurement activity across the region.
Sources: Gartner 2026 · European Commission · Deloitte DORA Survey 2025 · Fortinet 2026 · ECB Feb 2025 · IC Euro 2025

Section 2 · Industry Signals
Technology investment shifts, AI adoption patterns, and IT budget direction across ITCI's four core verticals.
2.1 · CPG & Retail · ~40% of ITCI Revenue
Signal
US retail technology budgets projected at $113B in 2026, +6.6% YoY (Forrester, 2026); software is 46% of total spend. CPG AI adoption: 71% of leaders adopted AI in at least one function in 2024, up from 42% in 2023 (McKinsey/LatentView, 2024); 89% using or piloting AI; 97% expect AI spend to increase next fiscal year (NVIDIA, Jan 2025). 100% of surveyed retailers have committed AI funding for 2025; 37.5% demonstrate advanced enterprise-wide capabilities (Valere, Mar 2025). SAP S/4HANA: 59% of SAP customers fully or partially live; 64% expect to be live or mid-migration by end-2025 (ASUG/Precisely, 2025). SAP consulting market exceeded $16B; rates rising 30–50% in 2026–27 (IC Euro, 2025). US retail media spend: $58.79B in 2025 rising to $69.33B in 2026 (eMarketer H2 2025). Tariffs tilting CPG toward cost-optimization and supply chain resilience IT while smaller discretionary projects slow (ISG, Mar 2025).
ITCI Lens
CPG and Retail has moved into a Day-2 AI phase: large clients have substantially built digital platforms and incremental investment is shifting toward intelligence layers — demand forecasting AI, supply chain optimization, retail media analytics, and agentic commerce. The 2027 SAP ECC deadline creates a mandatory migration wave with rising consulting rates and constrained talent supply. IT services firms with verified S/4HANA delivery credentials are in a structurally favorable demand position through 2027. Retail media network growth signals significant data engineering and closed-loop attribution work. Tariff-driven cost pressure is redirecting spend toward cost-optimization and supply chain resilience — direct adjacencies to ERP and analytics service lines.
2.2 · Manufacturing · ~25% of ITCI Revenue
Signal
Global manufacturing digital transformation spend projected at $224.7B in 2026, +13.8% YoY (ABI Research, 2026); Automotive leads at $87.3B. Industry 4.0 production status: 35% of manufacturers had AI in production in 2024, rising to ~50% by 2025, but only 5% achieved industrialized end-to-end digital operations (Capgemini-Microsoft, 2025). 172 WEF Lighthouse factories (Oct 2024) report an average 50% labour productivity boost. PLM cloud: global PLM market at $62.84B in 2026, 6.95% CAGR; cloud PLM holds 42% share growing at 8.1% CAGR (Custom Market Insights, 2026). 81% of industrial companies believe SaaS will become the standard for PLM (Aras, 2024). Siemens, PTC, and Dassault hold 80% combined PLM market share. Reshoring: 244,000 US manufacturing jobs in 2024; tariffs cited in 454% more reshoring decisions in 2025 vs 2024 (Reshoring Initiative, 2024).
ITCI Lens
Manufacturing has passed an inflection where roughly half of large manufacturers have AI in production, but only 5% have industrialized it. The gap between deployment and industrialization is where implementation and managed services work concentrates. Cloud PLM growing at 8.1% CAGR with 42% current share and 81% of industrial companies expecting SaaS to become standard represents a defined and active migration market. Reshoring-driven new high-tech manufacturing capacity creates greenfield plant IT demand across MES, IoT, and data infrastructure. Tariffs are redirecting manufacturing IT toward cost-optimization and automation rather than reducing overall spend.
2.3 · Travel & Hospitality · ~15% of ITCI Revenue
Signal
Airline IT spend reached $37B in 2024, up from $35B in 2023 (SITA 2024, 379 airlines); IT as % of airline revenue rose to 3.8%; 72% of airline IT executives expect further increases in 2025. Data and GenAI is the top investment priority for 56% of airlines. Hotel IT: CBRE reports hotel IT expenditures grew 4.6% in 2024 vs 4.0% total hotel expense growth (CBRE, Jul 2025); biggest increases in PMS, reservations, POS. PMS modernisation: Oracle OPERA Cloud saw 31%+ YoY increase in properties; Accor announced migration of its global portfolio across 110 countries to Oracle OPERA Cloud PMS (Oracle, Sep 2025). AI in production: IHG uses predictive housekeeping AI; Four Seasons Peninsula Papagayo — 50% buffet waste reduction in 8 months (BCG, Feb 2026); Hilton and Marriott deploying AI for pricing. Loyalty: 675M hotel loyalty members in 2024; program costs rising 4.4% annually vs 2.7% revenue growth — forcing platform redesign (Whitesky, Sep 2025). Legacy maintenance consumes 60–80% of T&H IT budgets (Rise Up Labs, 2026).
ITCI Lens
Both airlines and hotels are increasing IT spend above general expense growth trends. The sector is in a core-systems modernisation phase anchored by PMS, CRS, and loyalty platform replacement — Oracle OPERA Cloud is emerging as the consolidation standard, with large-scale migrations underway at major hotel groups. AI is past pilot in T&H: airlines fund AI for operational resilience; hotels deploy AI with quantified outcomes. Legacy maintenance at 60–80% of T&H IT budgets creates a structural modernisation backlog. Loyalty platform economics — costs rising 4.4% vs 2.7% revenue growth — force platform investment regardless of discretionary budget conditions.
2.4 · BFSI · ~10% of ITCI Revenue
Signal
Global banking IT spend: $176B in 2025, up from $167B in 2024 (EY Global Banking Outlook 2025); only 39% is change-the-bank; 61% is run-the-bank. Banking AI spend: $31.3B in 2024 growing to ~$40B in 2025 at 27% CAGR (Statista, 2025). 52% of financial services organisations actively using GenAI in 2024; but only 8% developing GenAI at strategic enterprise level — 78% remain tactical (IBM, Feb 2025). DORA compliance: 83% of European financial entities have formal cost estimates; 64% budgeting €2–5M per institution (Deloitte, 2025). 3,600+ German institutions responding (Fortinet, 2026). Basel IV required banks to redesign capital calculation engines. EU AI Act creates mandatory governance for AI in credit scoring. Core banking market: $17.94B in 2026. Middle East: GCC banks moved AI from pilot to production in 2025 (Mors Software, Dec 2025). Alibaba Cloud and Wio Bank MoU for AI-powered agents in Dubai (Oct 2025). BFSI is 23.8% of Middle East IT spend at 6.1% CAGR (Dataintelo, 2025).
ITCI Lens
BFSI IT budgets are growing but 61% remains in run-the-bank. Within the 39% change-the-bank portion, regulatory compliance — DORA, Basel IV, EU AI Act — is the least discretionary category: mandatory, time-bound, and formally budget-committed by 83% of European institutions. GenAI adoption is broad (52%) but industrialization is narrow (8%) — this gap represents the AI implementation and governance services opportunity. The Middle East BFSI market is growing faster than any other geography at 6.1% CAGR with local cloud infrastructure now deployable. Core banking modernisation at $17.94B reflects sustained multi-year program spend at mid-sized banks and digital entrants.

Emerging Market Patterns — FY2025–26
Market-level observations drawn from the signals in this report — not internal recommendations. These are directional patterns observable across geographies, industries, and competitive behaviour.
PATTERN 01
AI budgets remain protected across verticals
AI and automation spend is ring-fenced from discretionary IT budget cuts in CPG, Manufacturing, T&H, and BFSI. 97% of CPG/retail respondents expect AI spend to increase next fiscal year. Only 8% of banks are at strategic enterprise AI level — the gap between broad adoption and industrialization indicates sustained implementation demand.
PATTERN 02
Vendor consolidation is accelerating as a purchasing mechanism
$13B in IT contracts up for renewal in 2025, with 68% of CIOs planning a 20% reduction in vendor count. LTIM's largest deals explicitly involve consolidating multiple incumbent vendors into one. Enterprises are contracting their SI partner ecosystems, not expanding them — mid-tier firms that can demonstrate multi-service portfolio breadth are structurally better positioned than specialists.
PATTERN 03
Regulatory mandates creating non-discretionary, time-bound demand
DORA (Jan 2025), EU AI Act (Aug 2026 full compliance), KSA e-invoicing (Jul 2026), and Basel IV are all live forcing functions. 83% of European financial institutions have formal DORA cost estimates. These programs are mandatory, timeline-bound, and already budget-committed — they proceed regardless of macro conditions.
PATTERN 04
Mid-tier competitors are platformizing delivery narratives
LTIMindtree (BlueVerse, 300+ agents), Persistent Systems (SASVA, iAURA 2.0), Coforge (AgentSphere, 110+ agents), and Mphasis (NeoIP, Ontosphere) have all launched proprietary AI platforms in FY25–26. Platform ownership — not just AI capability — is becoming a prerequisite for large-deal credibility. The market is shifting from "we use AI" to "here are the outcomes our platform delivers."
PATTERN 05
AI-led TCV disclosure is emerging as a market benchmark
Mphasis: 60% of $2.12B FY26 TCV is AI-led. Coforge: AI embedded in all 14 large deals in FY25. LTIM: "AI-led deals" described as primary order flow driver. What was a differentiator in FY24 is becoming a baseline expectation in FY26. Competitive conversations increasingly require quantified AI-led deal percentages and client outcome metrics, not narrative positioning alone.
PATTERN 06
Sovereign cloud localization is an execution prerequisite in key geographies
Microsoft Saudi Azure (commercial availability 2026), AWS whole-of-government Australia (Jan 2025), and GCC bank AI deployments on local Dubai infrastructure are all live. In the Middle East and APAC, data-sovereign cloud is no longer a future consideration — it is a current procurement requirement. IT services firms without local cloud credentials or partnerships face structural access barriers in regulated-sector programs in these geographies.
PATTERN 07
M&A is functioning as an AI capability accelerator, not a scale mechanism
Coforge-Cigniti (AI quality assurance platform, BlueSwan), Mphasis-TAP (decision intelligence, Continuum AI), Infosys-Versent (AWS sovereign cloud credentials, ASX100 penetration), Capgemini-Cloud4C (Singapore managed services position) — all were relatively small valuations that added IP and credentials rather than headcount. The pattern: targeted acquisitions that immediately change a firm's go-to-market narrative and deal eligibility.
